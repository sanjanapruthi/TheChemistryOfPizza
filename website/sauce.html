Tomatoes

What is it?

The tomato is the edible, often red, fruit/berry of the plant Solanum lycopersicum, commonly known as a tomato plant. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae. The species originated in western South America.

Why is it red?

Green tomatoes are colored because of the presence of chlorophyll. As they ripen, the pigment lycopene develops. The presence of alternating double and single bonds allows it to absorb all but red light, causing the red color.

What causes the acidity?

Tomato sauce adds a "bright" flavor to a pizza. That brightness is the acidity found in tomatoes. Tomato acidity falls within the range of 4.3-4.9. Ripe tomatoes are less acidic because Acids are broken down in ripening fruits and this contributes to the sweeter rather than sharp tastes associated with unripe fruits.

How to neutralize the acidity?

Add a pinch of Baking Soda (Sodium Bicarbonate). The hydrogen ions will combine with the hydroxide ions in the baking soda, neutralizing the reaction and decreasing the overall acidity.

Garlic

What is it?

Garlic (Allium sativum) is a species in the onion genus, Allium.

What causes the sharp taste and pungent smell?

When a cell is broken by chopping, chewing, or crushing, enzymes stored in cell vacuoles trigger the breakdown of several sulfur-containing compounds. The resultant compounds are responsible for the sharp taste and strong smell of garlic.

What causes the hot taste?

Allicin has been found to be the compound most responsible for the "hot" sensation of raw garlic. The process of cooking garlic removes allicin, thus mellowing its spiciness. It is also the compound associated with bad breath.

Olive Oil

What is it?

Olive oil is mostly made up of triglycerides. These triglycerides have a variety of fatty acids attached to them. The most prevalent fatty acid is oleic acid.









Pepper

What is it?

Piperine is the primary ingredient that distinguishes black pepper. The chemical makeup of piperine is C17H19NO3.

What causes the smell?

Oils attribute to black pepper's aroma.

What causes the pungent taste?

The alkaloid chemical compound piperine creates the pungency.
